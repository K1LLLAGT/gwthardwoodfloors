FAQ

Questions homeowners ask us

Straight answers about what a hardwood project actually looks like — timelines, dust, odor, pets, and everything in between. Don't see your question? Just call.

How long does a typical refinishing job take?

Most whole-floor refinishes run about three to five days, depending on square footage, the stain, and the finish system. Sanding and staining happen first, then each coat of finish needs time to dry between passes. We'll give you a firm timeline once we've seen the floor.

Is hardwood refinishing very dusty?

Far less than people expect. We sand with professional, dust-contained equipment that captures the vast majority of dust at the source. You'll still want to wipe down a few surfaces afterward, but the days of a house buried in fine grit are behind us.

Will the finish smell? Can we stay in the house?

It depends on the finish you choose. Water-based systems are low-odor and clear quickly. Oil-based and traditional alcohol-based finishes carry a stronger smell and need more ventilation. For odor-sensitive households or occupied homes, we'll usually recommend a water-based system and walk you through what to expect.

What about my pets?

Pets and fresh finish don't mix — paws track and curiosity gets the better of them. We ask that pets stay out of the work area while floors are curing. We're happy to plan the job in sections when possible so your home stays livable.

Do you move the furniture?

We can help with the heavy pieces, but we ask that you clear small items, electronics, rugs, and breakables before we start. A clear room lets us prep properly and protects your belongings from dust and finish.

How soon can we walk on the floors and put furniture back?

You can usually walk on the floors in socks within a day of the final coat. We recommend waiting before putting rugs down and moving furniture back, since finishes keep curing to full hardness for a couple of weeks. We'll give you exact times for your specific finish.

Can you match a custom color to my cabinets or trim?

Yes — this is some of our favorite work. We blend stains and prove the color on sample boards milled from your own floor before we commit. Our WheatStone Oak finish in the portfolio came from exactly this process: matching a floor to two different cabinet tones in the same home.

How many times can a floor be refinished?

Solid hardwood can typically be refinished several times over its lifetime, because each refinish only removes a thin layer. Engineered floors have a thinner wear layer and can usually be refinished fewer times. We'll check your floor's thickness and let you know what's left to work with.

Should I refinish or replace my floors?

If the boards are solid and have enough thickness left, refinishing is almost always the better value — you keep the original wood and get a like-new result. Replacement makes sense when there's severe water damage, structural issues, or the wear layer is gone. We'll give you an honest read either way.

What's the difference between water-, oil-, and alcohol-based finishes?

Water-based finishes dry fast, stay clear, and keep your color true. Oil-based finishes add warmth and a slight amber tone and are very forgiving. Traditional alcohol-based (Swedish-style) finishes are prized for a hard, glass-clear film. Each has a place — we'll recommend the right one for your color, traffic, and household.

Can you repair water damage, pet stains, or a few bad boards?

Yes. We replace damaged boards, weave in new wood, and blend repairs into the surrounding floor so they disappear. A good patch matches species, grain, and board size — then the stain and finish are feathered so your eye never finds the seam.

Do you install new hardwood, or only refinish?

Both. We install new solid and engineered hardwood — including subfloor prep, acclimation, layout, and finishing — and we refinish existing floors and staircases. Many projects combine the two.

We're based in the Pontiac and Metro Detroit area and work with homeowners all over Michigan. If you're not sure whether you're in range, just give us a call.
