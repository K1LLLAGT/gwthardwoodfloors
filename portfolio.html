Case study · Bloomfield Hills

1,750 sq ft Red Oak Refinish — Nutmeg / White Mix

Residents of Isaac & Rachel wanted their main level to feel modern and high-end without fighting the home's existing woodwork. The catch: the kitchen cabinetry and the bar area cabinets sat at slightly different tones, and the new floor had to flatter both.

The challenge

Two cabinet tones, one floor

The biggest task on this job wasn't the sanding — it was the color. The kitchen cabinetry and the bar area cabinets each carried slightly different undertones, so a single off-the-shelf stain would have flattered one space and clashed with the other.

We built multiple sample boards from the home's own Red Oak and refined the mix step by step, checking each draw against both cabinet runs in the home's actual light until the floor color sat comfortably between them.

The custom formula
  • BaseDuraseal Nutmeg
  • BlendDuraseal Country White
  • Ratio1 : 1
  • TopcoatPallmann Ultra Matte
The final result

WheatStone Oak

A sophisticated, desaturated light-brown with gray, green-neutralizing, and soft pink-oak undertones. The finished floor reads modern and high-end, and — because it's neutralized rather than warm or cool — it complements both warm and cool interiors equally well.

It's the kind of color you can't pull from a single can. It came out of sample boards, patience, and a willingness to keep adjusting until both cabinet runs looked right next to the same floor.
