Services

Everything we do to a hardwood floor

From the first sanding pass to the final coat, we handle the whole job in-house. Here's what each service involves, when you need it, and why our family does it well.

01

Hardwood Floor Refinishing

Sand · stain · finish

What it is. We sand existing hardwood down to fresh, bare wood, repair what needs it, then apply new stain and a protective finish — essentially resetting your floor to new.

When it's needed. Worn traffic paths, dull or flaking finish, surface scratches, pet damage, water marks, or simply a color you've outgrown. Most solid hardwood can be refinished several times over its life.

Why GWT. Clean sanding is the whole game. We grade through the right abrasive sequence so the floor takes stain evenly — no swirl, no drum marks, no blotch — which is what separates a floor that looks redone from one that just looks recoated.
02

Hardwood Floor Installation

Solid & engineered

What it is. Laying new hardwood — solid or engineered — including subfloor prep, acclimation, layout, fastening, and finishing.

When it's needed. New construction, additions, replacing carpet or tile, or pulling up flooring that's too far gone to refinish.

Why GWT. We let the wood acclimate, set proper expansion gaps, and plan board layout so the room reads right from the doorway. A floor installed with patience stays flat and tight for decades.
03

Staircase Refinishing

Treads · risers · rails

What it is. Refinishing or rebuilding stair treads, risers, and handrails so the staircase matches the floors it connects to.

When it's needed. Stairs take more wear than any floor in the house. When the finish wears through on the nosing or the color no longer matches a freshly refinished floor, the stairs need attention too.

Why GWT. Stairs are detailed, hand-worked surfaces — lots of edges, no room for shortcuts. We match the floor's stain and sheen exactly so the transition looks like it was always one piece.
04

Custom Stain Mixing

Sample boards · hand-blended

What it is. Blending two or more stains to create a color that's yours — then proving it on sample boards milled from your actual floor before we commit.

When it's needed. When an off-the-shelf color doesn't tie into your cabinets, trim, or lighting. Most high-end results come from a custom blend, not a single can.

Why GWT. This is our signature. The WheatStone Oak finish in our portfolio was a 1:1 Nutmeg and Country White blend developed over several sample boards to sit right with two different cabinet tones. We don't guess on color — we prove it.
05

Bleaching

Lightening & tone control

What it is. Chemically lightening wood to remove deep color, even out the natural background, or reach pale, modern tones that stain alone can't deliver.

When it's needed. Going light or white-washed on a naturally warm species like oak, neutralizing yellow or pink undertones, or lifting old water and pet stains before refinishing.

Why GWT. Bleaching is unforgiving — uneven application shows forever. We control timing, neutralization, and grain raise so the wood ends up clean and ready to take a light stain evenly.
06

Water-based Finishing

Low odor · fast cure · clear

What it is. A clear, durable topcoat that dries fast and stays color-true, keeping light woods and cool tones from ambering over time.

When it's needed. When you want the floor to keep its exact color, need lower odor and quicker return-to-service, or are finishing in an occupied home.

Why GWT. We finish with professional systems like Pallmann, Bona, and Loba and apply in controlled coats — the fast cure window rewards a steady, experienced hand.
07

Alcohol-based Finishing

Hard-wearing · classic clarity

What it is. A traditional Swedish-style finish system known for a hard, glass-clear film with exceptional clarity and depth.

When it's needed. When you want the classic, time-tested look and a tough surface — often specified for its clarity over fine grain.

Why GWT. We've worked with Glitsa-style systems for years. These finishes demand ventilation, timing, and respect — exactly the kind of detailed work our family was built on.
08

Oil-based Finishing

Warm · rich · forgiving

What it is. A classic polyurethane topcoat that adds warmth and a slight amber glow, building a rich, durable film over the wood.

When it's needed. When you want that traditional warm tone, especially on red oak and darker stains, and a forgiving, well-proven finish.

Why GWT. Oil-based finishes flow and level beautifully when applied right. We control coats and dry times so you get the warmth without runs, bubbles, or uneven sheen.
09

Repairs & Patching

Board swaps · blending

What it is. Replacing damaged boards, weaving in new wood, and blending repairs into the surrounding floor so they disappear.

When it's needed. Water damage, deep gouges, pet stains, failed previous repairs, or transitions left behind by removed walls and old registers.

Why GWT. A good patch is invisible. We match species, grain direction, and board size, then feather the stain and finish so your eye never catches the seam.
