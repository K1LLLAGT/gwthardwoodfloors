Floor Care Guide

How to keep your floors looking new

A good hardwood floor rewards a little routine. Follow these simple habits and your finish will stay beautiful for years between refinishes — whether your floors are brand new or freshly restored.

Step 01 Daily & weekly cleaning

The goal is simple: keep grit off the surface, because fine dirt is what dulls a finish over time.

  • Daily / as needed: Sweep or dry dust-mop high-traffic areas. A microfiber mop lifts grit without scratching.
  • Weekly: Vacuum with a hard-floor (bare-floor) setting or a soft-brush head — never a beater bar.
  • Weekly or as needed: Damp-mop with a cleaner made for hardwood. Use a well-wrung microfiber pad — the floor should look barely damp and dry within seconds.
  • Spills: Wipe up promptly. Standing liquid is the enemy of any wood floor.

Step 02 What to avoid

Most floor damage we're called to fix comes from the wrong cleaning method, not normal wear.

Do

  • Use cleaners made specifically for hardwood floors
  • Keep the mop barely damp and dry quickly
  • Test any new product in a closet or corner first
  • Follow your finish manufacturer's recommendations

Avoid

  • Steam mops — heat and moisture can cloud or lift a finish
  • Wet mopping or letting water pool on the floor
  • Vinegar, ammonia, bleach, or all-purpose sprays
  • Oil soaps and wax on a polyurethane finish
  • Vacuum beater bars and stiff-bristle brooms

Step 03 How to protect your floors

A few small habits prevent the scratches and dents that send floors to early refinishing.

  • Rugs & mats: Place walk-off mats at every entry to catch grit and road salt — important through a Michigan winter. Use breathable, non-staining rug pads (avoid solid rubber backing that can mar the finish).
  • Furniture pads: Put felt pads under all furniture legs and check them regularly — a worn pad with embedded grit acts like sandpaper.
  • Pet nails: Keep nails trimmed. Even a well-finished floor will show scratches from long claws over time.
  • Sunlight: Wood color shifts with UV exposure. Rotate rugs and area pieces occasionally so the floor ages evenly, and consider blinds for sun-heavy rooms.
  • Humidity: Wood expands and contracts with the seasons. Aim to keep indoor humidity in a stable, moderate range year-round to minimize gapping in winter and cupping in summer.
  • Shoes: Keep heels in good repair and grit outside — small habits add up over years.

Step 04 When to call for refinishing or repairs

Catching wear early often means a simpler, less expensive fix. Reach out when you notice:

  • Dull, worn finish in traffic paths, hallways, or in front of the sink and stove
  • Surface scratches that have started reaching bare wood
  • Gray or black spots, which can mean moisture has gotten past the finish
  • Boards that are cupping, crowning, or have noticeable gaps
  • Water damage, pet stains, or a few boards that need replacing
  • A color that no longer fits your space — a refinish is the time to change it

When a finish is worn but the wood underneath is sound, a recoat or refinish restores protection before damage reaches the wood. We're glad to take a look and tell you honestly what your floor needs.
